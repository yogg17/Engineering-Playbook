Once upon a time, in a bright green forest, lived a little bear named Barnaby. Barnaby was a very happy bear, but he had one big problem. He lost his favorite red boot!
"Oh no," Barnaby said, tapping his bare furry foot on the soft grass. "Where could it be?"
He decided to go on a grand adventure to find it. First, he walked to the tall, wiggly-waggly grass. Swish, swish, swish went the grass against his tummy. Barnaby looked down. He did not see his boot, but he did see a tiny green frog.
"Ribbit!" said the frog. "Are you looking for this?" The frog hopped over a round yellow leaf. But under the leaf, there was only a smooth brown stone.
"No," Barnaby sighed. "That is a pretty stone, but it is not my boot." He waved goodbye to the frog. Wave, wave, wave.
Next, Barnaby walked to the big, old oak tree. Crunch, crunch, crunch went the dry twigs under his paws. He looked up into the branches. He saw a brown squirrel with a big, fluffy tail.
"Chirp, chirp!" said the squirrel. "Are you looking for this?" The squirrel pointed to a little hole in the tree. Barnaby peeked inside. He saw a pile of crunchy brown acorns.
"No," Barnaby smiled. "Those are yummy acorns, but they are not my boot." He waved goodbye to the squirrel.
Barnaby’s tummy gave a loud rumble. Rumble, rumble! He was getting tired and hungry. He walked back to his cozy wooden house. He sat down on the front porch and big, fat tears filled his eyes.
Just then, his friend Bella the bunny hopped into the yard. Hop, hop, hop! She was carrying something bright and red.
"Barnaby! Barnaby!" Bella called out. "Look what I found by the sparkling blue river!"
Barnaby looked up. It was his red boot!
"Hooray!" shouted Barnaby. He clapped his paws together. Clap, clap, clap!
He slid his furry foot right inside the boot. It fit perfectly. Snug, snug, snug. Barnaby was so happy that he did a little bear dance. He wiggled his ears, shook his tail, and stomped his feet. Stomp, stomp, stomp!
"Thank you, Bella!" Barnaby said. He gave his friend a big, warm bear hug.
That night, Barnaby ate a big bowl of sweet yellow honey. He brushed his white teeth. Then, he climbed into his soft, warm bed. He did not leave his red boots on the floor. He put them right next to his pillow so they would be safe. He closed his heavy eyes, took a deep breath, and fell fast asleep. Snore, snore, snore.
Goodnight, Barnaby!
